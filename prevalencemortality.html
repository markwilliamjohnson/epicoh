How to Interpret

  • Incidence (I) adds new CHD cases from the disease‑free population. Lower with healthier lifestyle and primary prevention.
  • Mortality (M) removes people from the CHD pool. Better treatment quality reduces M among those with CHD.
  • Prevalence (P) changes as: Pt+1 = Pt + I·(1 − Pt) − M·Pt.
  • Paradox: mortality can drop (treatment ↑) while prevalence stays flat if incidence also drops (lifestyle & primary prevention ↑).
Model: I = incidenceBase × lifestyleFactor × (1 − primaryPrevention), M = mortalityRate × (1 − treatmentQuality).
