What makes this world weird?

Each frame we apply baseline Newtonian forces, then roll the dice for rule-breaking events.

  • Gravity anomaly: g temporarily flips, fades, or points sideways.
  • Impulse glitch: random impulse is applied (mean zero, finite variance).
  • Time stutter: the integrator randomly reuses the last state (as if time hiccuped).
  • Coulomb wobble: inverse-square law becomes inverse-cube or sign-flips briefly.

Despite local chaos, the randomizers are mean-zero or mean-reverting, so long-run averages trend toward Newton’s laws ("population studies"). Adjust probabilities and intensity to explore departures.

Keyboard: Space pause/step, A drop apple, C spawn charge.
